## 2. DESIGN SPECIFICATIONS

### Implementation Flexibility

**Claude Code has creative latitude to:**
- Adjust layout and visual hierarchy for better flow
- Edit copy for length/rhythm if needed for design (preserve meaning and tone)
- Determine optimal number and placement of images
- Make typographic decisions within the guidelines
- Reorganize content within sections if it improves the experience
- **Pull additional quotes from interview artifacts if more content is needed** (see working document for full interview quotes)

**The goal is a cohesive, warm, inviting page - not rigid adherence to this brief.**

### Visual Hierarchy
- **Section 1 (Hero):** Large, breathing whitespace. Quote dominates. Image intimate.
- **Section 2:** Clear readable paragraphs, medium size, integrated quote stands out slightly
- **Section 3:** Service cards visually distinct but not overwhelming. Prices visible but not dominant.
- **Section 4:** Photo + flowing text, no CV formatting
- **Section 5:** Lighter visual weight than main sections
- **Section 6:** Single prominent button/link

### Typography
- **Maximum 2 fonts:**
  - Font 1: For quotes (character, warmth, possibly handwritten feel) - must support Portuguese accents
  - Font 2: For body text (clean, readable) - must support Portuguese accents (á, ã, ç, etc.)
  
- **Quote styling:** Italics or distinct font, larger than body, with attribution "— Fabiana"
- **Body text:** Clean, readable, generous line height
- **Headings:** Simple, not overly bold or corporate

### Color Palette
- **Primary:** Earth tones, warm (terracotta, ochre, warm browns)
- **NOT:** Clinical white/blue, spa green/aqua, corporate grays
- **Accent:** Warm accent color for links/buttons
- **Background:** Warm off-white or very light cream, NOT pure white

### Spacing
- **Cosy and warm ("aconchegante")** - NOT overly spacious or minimalist
- Comfortable reading width, not stretched across full viewport
- Sections feel connected, not floating in whitespace
- Margins appropriate but not excessive
- Reading flow should feel intimate and welcoming, not sparse

**IMPORTANT:** Avoid the tendency toward excessive whitespace. The site should feel warm and inhabited, not gallery-like or overly minimal.

### Images
- **Only authentic images:** Bia, her hands, her space
- **NO stock photos** under any circumstances
- **Style:** Natural light, intimate but professional, warm tones
- **Placement:** Hero image full-width or large, Section 4 image portrait-style

### Responsive/Mobile
- **Mobile-first approach** (most Portuguese traffic on mobile)
- Service cards stack vertically on mobile
- Text remains readable without zooming
- Touch targets (links/buttons) appropriately sized

The new homepage should:
